SOME INFO ABOUT US


Two materials that are frequently used to make plastic bottles are polypropylene (PP) and polyethylene terephthalate (PET). PP bottles are perfect for food storage, medicinal containers, and reusable water bottles since they are strong, lightweight, and chemical-resistant. They are renowned for being reasonably priced and heat-resistant. On the other hand, because of its robustness, clarity, and capacity to be recycled, PET bottles are frequently utilized as single-use water and beverage containers. Additionally, PET is more translucent than PP, increasing the product's visibility. PET is more likely to be recycled than PP, however both can be recycled. PET is stronger and more impact-resistant than PP, which is often more flexible. While both materials are safe to use in food and drink applications, PET is typically preferred due to its superior barrier qualities, which protect the product from oxygen and moisture.
